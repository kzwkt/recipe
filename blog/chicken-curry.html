Chicken Curry

A rich and aromatic chicken curry that's perfect over rice or with naan bread. This recipe focuses on creating a flavorful sauce with tender chicken pieces.

---

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (vegetable, canola, or ghee)
  • 500g (approx. 1.1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

For the Curry Base:

  • 1 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp minced fresh ginger
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 (14.5 oz / 400g) can diced tomatoes (undrained)
  • 1 cup (240ml) chicken broth or water
  • ¾ cup (180ml) full-fat coconut milk (from a can)

Spices (Adjust to your taste):

  • 2 tbsp curry powder (mild or medium, depending on preference)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • ½ tsp garam masala (add at the end)

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
---

Instructions

  1. Prepare Chicken:

    Pat the chicken pieces dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper.

    Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Add chicken pieces in a single layer (cook in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding).

    Sear the chicken for 3-4 minutes per side until lightly browned. It doesn't need to be cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

  2. Sauté Aromatics:

    Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil (or ghee) to the same pan.

    Add the chopped onion and cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.

    Add minced ginger and garlic. Cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.

  3. Bloom Spices:

    Stir in the curry powder, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute, stirring constantly, until very fragrant. This "blooming" step deepens the spice flavors.

  4. Build the Sauce:

    Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute, stirring.

    Add the canned diced tomatoes (with their juice), chicken broth, and coconut milk. Stir well to combine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.

    Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer.

  5. Simmer the Curry:

    Return the seared chicken pieces (along with any accumulated juices) to the pan.

    Reduce heat to low, cover the pan, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. The sauce should have thickened slightly.

    Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. Finish and Serve:

    Stir in the garam masala. This spice blend is usually added at the end for its aromatic qualities.

    Taste the curry and adjust salt and pepper if needed. You can also add a squeeze of fresh lime juice for brightness if desired.

    Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

---

Serving Suggestions

  • Steamed Basmati or Jasmine rice
  • Warm naan bread or roti
  • A side of plain yogurt or raita (yogurt with cucumber and mint)
